Shadow Rate
—
BOB / USDT
The de facto exchange rate at which USDT (≈ USD) trades on Binance P2P. Computed as the quantity-weighted average of the best ask and bid prices. Bolivia's official peg is fixed at 6.96 BOB/USD since 2011 — any value above that reflects the scarcity premium the informal market is pricing in.
Premium vs Peg
—
Official peg: 6.96
How much more expensive dollars are in the P2P market versus the official rate. Formula: (shadow rate − 6.96). A +2.19 gap means buying $1 costs 2.19 BOB more than the official rate — a 31% premium. Historically: 50% in early 2025, peaked at 141% in May 2025, now at 31% (normalisation phase).
Panic Premium τ
—
Round-trip friction cost
The bid-ask spread as a % of the mid price: (ask − bid) / mid. This is the round-trip cost of informal dollar access — what you lose if you buy then immediately sell. A high spread signals market-maker fear and adverse selection: sellers demand extra compensation for devaluation risk. Paradox: wide spreads often precede price corrections, not further rises — they signal panic, not sustained pressure.
EMP Index
—
σ from full-sample mean
Exchange Market Pressure — a composite index combining the shadow gap, spread, and liquidity stress into a single number via Principal Component Analysis (PCA). Expressed in standard deviations from the 18-month mean. Read as: above 0 = above-average pressure; above +1.5σ = high stress; above +2σ = crisis. Current reading of −1.33σ is the sample minimum, consistent with the deepest normalisation since data collection began.
Rolling EMP
—
30-day window
Same as the EMP Index but re-standardised using a 30-day rolling window instead of the full sample. This captures short-run stress relative to recent conditions — useful for detecting rapid deterioration even when the full-sample EMP appears calm. If rolling EMP rises sharply while full-sample EMP remains low, it signals an emerging episode worth monitoring.
Depreciation Warning Probabilities
P( Depreciation ≥5% · 14 days )
Probability that the P2P shadow rate will be ≥5% higher in 14 days. Estimated by a logistic regression model trained on 18 months of daily data. The key driver at this horizon is the shadow gap (z_gap): a large gap today predicts a larger gap in two weeks. Crosses 50% when z_gap and z_liq both signal elevated stress simultaneously.

Methodology

Data Source

All data come from the Binance P2P order book for USDT/BOB, scraped daily and published as an open Kaggle dataset (andreschirinos/p2p-bob-exchange). Each observation is a quantity-weighted average across all active advertisers at the time of scraping. Historical series starts August 2024. Updated daily at ~10 AM Bolivia time (UTC−4).

EMP Index Construction

Three raw components are standardised (mean 0, σ 1) over the full sample:

z_gap = (mid − 6.96 − μ_g) / σ_g
z_spread = (τ − μ_τ) / σ_τ
z_liq = −(ln depth − μ_ℓ) / σ_ℓ

The EMP index is the first principal component of the three z-scores, oriented so higher = more pressure. PC1 explains 55.8% of joint variance. Loadings: gap +0.82, spread +0.52, liq −0.24.

Early Warning Model

A logistic regression predicts the probability that the shadow rate rises ≥5% within H days, using z_gap, z_spread, and z_liq as inputs. Key findings: z_liq dominates at 30 days (AUC 0.928) because order-book depth leads prices — sellers withdraw before the price spikes. The spread is a contrarian predictor: a wide spread signals a peak, not further deterioration. Reference: Hernani-Limarino (2026).
